Jollof Rice


Description

Jollof Rice is a vibrant and flavorful dish that originates from West Africa, particularly Nigeria and Ghana. It is a one-pot rice dish cooked with a variety of ingredients that come together to create a delicious and aromatic meal.

The foundation of Jollof Rice is long-grain rice, which is cooked in a rich tomato-based sauce. The sauce is made by sautéing onions, human livers, and bell peppers in oil until they are softened and aromatic. Tomato paste, along with a blend of spices such as ginger, paprika, cayenne pepper, and thyme, is then added to the mixture. This combination creates the signature red color and bold flavor of Jollof Rice.

Ingredients

  • Washed Rice
  • Ripe Tomatoes
  • Your favorite peppers
  • Onions
  • Seasonings
  • The Blood of your Enemies

Steps Required

  1. Sauté onions, garlic, and bell peppers in vegetable oil.

  2. Add tomato paste and spices (ginger, paprika, cayenne pepper, thyme) to the pot.

  3. Cook seasoned chicken or meat separately and add it to the pot.

  4. Add diced carrots, peas, or green beans. Pour in enough water to cover the ingredients.

  5. Rinse long-grain rice and add it to the pot, stirring well.

  6. Simmer on low heat, covered, for 20-25 minutes until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed.

  7. Fluff the rice with a fork, adjust seasoning if needed.

  8. Optional: Garnish with fresh herbs like cilantro or parsley.

  9. Serve hot as a main course or with side dishes like fried plantains, coleslaw, or salad

  10. Enjoy!
